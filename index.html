Why this matters

Why metadata-only provenance breaks down.

Roadscript focuses on content-layer authenticity because screenshots, reposts, edits, and normal platform handling do not preserve fragile file-level provenance by default.

Metadata can be stripped

File-level context can disappear during export, compression, reposting, and platform processing.

Screenshots break provenance

Screen-captured or transformed copies make it harder to rely on external provenance alone.

AI-generated and edited media is harder to verify

Audiences increasingly need clearer ways to evaluate authenticity beyond source claims or visual intuition.

Media needs authenticity at the content layer

Creators, platforms, and users need signals designed to travel with the media itself.

FAQ

Key questions, answered briefly.

What is Cyphra?
Cyphra is the user-facing app for verifying Roadscript-authenticated media. It is designed to make authenticity checks simple for creators, users, and developers.
How is Roadscript different from metadata?
Metadata lives beside the file and can often be stripped, edited, or lost. Roadscript focuses on content-layer authenticity signals that are embedded directly into the media itself.
Does it survive screenshots?
Screenshot survival is one of the key robustness goals. The Roadscript engine is being designed and tested around transformations such as screenshots, recompression, and resizing.
Are images uploaded to a server?
The roadmap prioritizes local-first processing where possible. Verification should not require unnecessary media uploads unless a future cloud feature explicitly needs it.

About Roadscript

Engineering authenticity infrastructure with a user-facing verification layer.

Roadscript is an independent media authenticity initiative focused on embedding invisible signals directly into content. Cyphra is the user-facing verification product layer designed to help people interpret whether Roadscript-authenticated media still carries a valid signal. The current website and demo are public previews intended to communicate the roadmap, product direction, and engineering priorities while packaged releases and deeper integrations continue to mature.
